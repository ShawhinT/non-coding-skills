# Naming Playbook

How to name (or rename) an offer so it actually pulls. Based on Hormozi's $100M Offers naming chapter.

## The core idea

Naming is "putting a bow on the puppy." You're not changing the work, the delivery, or the price — you're changing the wrapper around the same Grand Slam Offer. The same offer can pull 2–10x more responses just by changing the name. Offers fatigue over time; you refresh them by renaming the wrapper, not rebuilding the offer.

The job of the name is simple: make the right person stop and think, *"That's for me, right now."*

## The M.A.G.I.C. formula

A name can include up to five components:

- **M — Magnetic reason to care.** Promo or angle (free, new, advanced, private, founding, exclusive, etc.).
- **A — Avatar.** Who it's for (founder, CEO, surgeon, agency owner).
- **G — Goal.** The clear business result (not the tool).
- **I — Interval.** Realistic duration (30-day, 6-week, weekend).
- **C — Container.** A word from the container library (see below) that signals "bundled system."

You don't need all five every time, but the more you stack, the more specific and compelling it feels. 3–5 components is usually right. Fewer feels generic; more gets unwieldy.

**Formula:** `[Reason to care] + [Avatar] + [Outcome] + [Timeframe] + [Container]`

## Container words

Always finish with a container word. The container tells the buyer "this is a bundled system, not a random hour of your time," which makes it harder to compare to commodity coaching.

Pick a container that matches both the **format** (1 day, 6 weeks, ongoing) and the **feel** (intense, deep, transformative, structured).

**Speed + depth family** (good for short premium engagements):
intensive, sprint, deep dive, accelerator, fast track, shortcut, blitz

**Structured program family** (good for multi-week programs):
program, bootcamp, incubator, masterclass, blueprint, system, solution, reset

**Event family** (good for one-time gatherings):
workshop, summit, experience, mastermind, launch

**Transformation family** (good for identity-shift offers):
challenge, transformation, makeover

For a 1:1 high-ticket Claude engagement that's fast and intensive, the right container is from the *speed + depth* family. "Session" is too vague — never use it as the container word.

## The 7 naming principles

1. **The name must SELL, not impress.** Judge names by response, not by how clever they sound to you. Lazy naming kills conversions; the same offer gets 2–10x the response purely from a better name.

2. **Treat the name as wrapping paper, not the present.** Don't rebuild the program every time you're bored. Do rename it periodically when the market gets numb to the old name.

3. **Use 3–5 M.A.G.I.C. components.** Avatar + Goal makes the outcome and who it's for obvious. Interval shows duration. Reason + Container make it feel like a special, bundled thing. If the name doesn't communicate *who it's for + what they get + roughly how long*, it's weak.

4. **Always finish with a container word.** Signals structure and completeness. Raises perceived value.

5. **Name to trigger "that's me" in their brain.** Implicit-egotism effect: people are drawn to things that resemble them. Use labels they already use for themselves. Mirror the conversation already going on in their head. If your avatar calls themselves a "founder," don't say "executive."

6. **Clarity beats cleverness, every time.** If they can't instantly tell what it is and why to care, you lost. Sacrifice cute and witty for clear avatar + clear outcome + clear container. Use clever only after clarity is locked in and tested.

7. **Don't debate names in your head — test them in the wild.** Generate a bunch using M.A.G.I.C. Shortlist a few that are clearest. Run them in content, emails, and DMs. Keep the one that actually pulls. The market decides.

## Working with Shaw on naming

When Shaw needs help naming an offer:

1. Confirm the M.A.G.I.C. components (Avatar, Goal, Interval, Container) before generating any options.
2. Generate 8–10 candidates grouped into 3–4 directions (e.g., "AI Native framing," "outcome-first," "mechanism-first," "identity-first"). Variety > volume.
3. Pick a top 3 and explain *why* each is your top pick — different reasons for different angles (clarity, brand identity, outcome strength).
4. Default to clarity over cleverness. If a candidate is cute but unclear, drop it.
5. Remind Shaw at the end: *the market decides*. Pick the one he'd be least embarrassed to paste into a DM tomorrow, ship it, and iterate based on real responses.

## A real example

For the "Onboard Claude" 30-day intensive, the M.A.G.I.C. inputs were:
- Avatar: Founder
- Goal: Onboard Claude / 20–40 hrs back / first AI hire
- Interval: 30-day
- Container: Intensive (speed + depth family, matches the white-glove premium feel)

The shortlist included:
- "The AI Native Founder Intensive" (identity-first, brand-building)
- "Hire Your First Claude Agent: 30-Day Founder Intensive" (mechanism-first, most concrete)
- "The 30-Day Claude Intensive for Founders" (boring but bulletproof)
- "Onboard Claude: A 30-Day 1:1 Intensive for Founders" (verb mirrors the mechanism, clearest who+what+how-long)

Shaw picked the last one because "onboard" matches the mechanism perfectly and "1:1 Intensive" signals premium + bundled in three words.
