For founder-CEOs who know AI matters but are too busy running the business to figure it out — and want to finally delegate the boring stuff to AI without spending nights on YouTube or learning a new tool.

## Key decisions made during drafting (worth remembering)

- **Headline iteration:** Started with a long version including "without building a single automation." Shaw flagged that automation framing wasn't relevant. Revised to "Onboard Claude In 30 Days And Delete 20–40 Hours Of Busy Work Every Month." The verb "delete" (visceral) beat "save" or "get back" (soft). The number "20–40 hours" matches the floor of the outcome guarantee — important for consistency.

- **Subhead:** Locked the first half ("For founder-CEOs who know AI matters but are too busy running the business to figure it out") and iterated separately on the second half. Lesson: when iterating subheads, split them in half — the first half names the avatar, the second half makes the promise + anti-effort anchor.

- **Qualifier bullets:** Critical addition was the "you're already using Claude but you don't feel like you're anywhere close to maximizing it" bullet. Shaw added this after referencing a real customer call where this pain came up explicitly. Lesson: always weave in customer-language bullets when Shaw shares context from real conversations.

- **Promise:** Originally said "no automations, no tech stack, no learning a new tool." Shaw refined to "no multi-month builds, no external contractors, no learning new tools." The contractor pain was very specific and visceral — worth more than abstract anti-effort anchors.

- **Bonuses:** Started with 5 bonuses, cut to 2. Lesson: Shaw prefers a leaner stack early (he's still learning what objections actually come up). The two surviving bonuses each kill a clear next-problem (Starter Skill Pack → "where do I start," Team Handoff Protocol → "how do I scale beyond me").

- **Step 3 of the process:** Initially drafted as a weak "stress-test and lock-in" session. I pushed back on whether it earned its keep against the value equation. Shaw considered cutting it entirely, then proposed reframing it as a "next 30/60/90 playbook" session. We evaluated that against the value equation (↑ dream outcome, ↑ likelihood, ↓ effort) and it cleared the bar. Became "The Roadmap." Lesson: a weak step is worse than no step — but reframing a weak step around an unanswered objection often saves it.

- **Guarantee:** Two-tier with a 20-hour floor (matching the bottom of the headline range, not the average). Shaw specifically wanted to keep the "Session 1 wage guarantee" because it kills the highest-friction objection (busy executives worrying about wasting their first 2 hours).

- **Voice corrections from Shaw during drafting:**
  - "Lighten up on the em-dashes"
  - "Focus everything around Claude — no ChatGPT"
  - "Use 'onboarding Claude' instead of 'teaching Claude'"
  - "Working on the business instead of working in the business"

These became part of the voice rules in the main SKILL.md.
