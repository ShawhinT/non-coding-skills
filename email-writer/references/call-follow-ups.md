# Call Follow-Ups

Patterns for following up after a research call, expert network sync, or discovery call. These emails confirm alignment, provide next steps, and keep momentum.

Read this file before drafting any post-call follow-up email.

---

## Structure

Shaw's call follow-ups follow a consistent 4-part structure:

1. **Warm opener** — "Really enjoyed our call yesterday!" (or similar). Always genuine, never filler.
2. **Compliment their idea** — One sentence reacting to something specific from the call. Use "Loved your [concise 3-4 word description]." Keep the description tight — don't over-explain.
3. **Value / recap** — Provide something useful: a talk outline, a resource, a summary of what was agreed. This is the meat of the email.
4. **Closing question** — End with a single question to prompt a reply. "What do you think?" is the default.

**Sign-off:** "Cheers, Shaw" (these are warm, peer-level relationships post-call).

---

## Template: Workshop/Talk Follow-Up

**When to use:** After a call where Shaw and the person agreed on a talk topic for an event series. The goal is to confirm the direction and share a narrative structure.

**Subject line:** "Call follow-up"

```
Hey [First Name],

Really enjoyed our call yesterday!

Loved your [3-4 word description of their talk idea]. I think this would be super valuable to the target audience for this series.

Here's a general talk outline that works well:
- About you
- Status quo (i.e. how [their specific domain] is done now)
- Problem (i.e. why the status quo sucks)
- Solution (i.e. how AI helps)
- Demo or details on solution
- Q&A

What do you think?

Cheers,
Shaw
```

**Personalization rules for the compliment line:**
- Keep the idea description to 3-4 words. Concise beats precise.
- React to their IDEA, not their credentials.
- Good: "Loved your agent skills for analytics talk idea"
- Good: "Loved your metric creation use case"
- Good: "Loved your talk idea on how AI is changing data platforms"
- Bad: "Loved your impressive background in data engineering and your thoughts on metric creation" (too long, evaluative)

**Talk outline customization:**
- The status quo parenthetical should reference their specific domain (e.g., "how business metrics are built now", "how analytics tasks are done now", "how data platforms are managed now")
- Keep it generic enough that it works as a starting framework, not a finished plan
- The outline is a bulleted list — this is one of the allowed exceptions to the "no bullet points" rule

## Template: General Call Follow-Up

**When to use:** After a discovery call, sales call, or research call where the next step is NOT a talk outline.

**Subject line:** "Call follow-up"

```
Hey [First Name],

Really enjoyed our call [yesterday/on Thursday/etc.]!

[COMPLIMENT — one sentence reacting to something specific from the call]

[VALUE — recap of agreed next step, resource, or info you promised to send]

[CLOSING QUESTION — one question to move things forward]

Cheers,
Shaw
```

---

## Threading Rules

- **Default:** Create as a reply in the existing email thread using `threadId`.
- **Exception:** If the last message in the thread is a Gmail emoji reaction (e.g., a heart or thumbs up), create the follow-up as a **fresh new thread** with no `threadId`. Gmail treats emoji reactions as messages, and replying to them causes rendering issues.
- **Subject line:** Always "Call follow-up" for post-call emails, whether threaded or fresh.

---

## Batch Follow-Ups

When following up with multiple people after calls (e.g., after a day of back-to-back research calls):

- **Use a consistent template.** Don't force variation across recipients. The same structure with swapped personalization is better than 5 different email styles.
- **Only customize:** the greeting name, the compliment line, and the status quo parenthetical in the outline.
- **Present the template to Shaw first** before creating drafts, so he can approve the structure and tweak wording. Then batch-create all drafts at once.
