# Expert Network Replies

Patterns for responding to AI Builder Academy (ABA) expert network newsletter replies. These are people who signed up for Shaw's expert network and responded to a newsletter asking for workshop leaders, collaborators, or participants.

Read this file before drafting any reply to an expert network respondent.

---

## Context

Shaw sends newsletters to his ABA expert network asking for specific types of collaborators (e.g., "Looking for analytics workshop leaders (April)"). People reply with varying levels of interest, fit, and clarity. Shaw needs to respond quickly and route each person to the right next step.

---

## Response Categories

### 1. Good Fit → Book a Call

**When to use:** The person expressed interest, their background aligns with what was asked for, and you want to move forward.

**Template:**

```
Hey [First Name],

Thanks for your reply! [OPTIONAL PERSONALIZATION LINE]

Let's hop on a quick call where I can share more details about the event series, and we can talk through ideas.

Do any of these times work?
[CALENDLY LINK]

Looking forward to it :)

Cheers,
Shaw
```

**Personalization rules:**
- Only add a personalization line if the person gave you something specific to react to
- React to what they DID or SAID, not who they are
- Examples of earned personalization:
  - "Love these ideas." (they pitched specific workshop angles)
  - "Love the way you're thinking about this." (they offered to create a one-pager or showed thoughtful framing)
  - "Good to hear from you." (you have an existing relationship with this person)
- If their reply was short/generic ("Let's do it!", "sounds interesting", "happy to hear more"), skip the personalization line entirely and go straight to "Let's hop on a quick call..."

**Handling constraints people mention:**
- If they mention timing concerns, fold it into the call purpose: "talk through timing/ideas" instead of "talk through ideas"
- Do NOT write "Totally understand [constraint]" — it's empty validation
- Do NOT write "No rush" unless you actually have no timeline pressure

### 2. Interested But Timing Uncertain → Soft Invite With Context

**When to use:** The person is interested but flagged a constraint (needs time to decide, schedule is tight, etc.) AND you have a real timeline.

**Template:**

```
Hey [First Name],

Thanks for your reply! [AFFIRM THEIR VALUE — reference what makes them relevant]

[STATE YOUR TIMELINE — be transparent about your constraints]

[GRACEFUL OUT — leave the door open for future opportunities]

Cheers,
Shaw
```

**Example (Sharat — asked for 2 weeks to decide):**

```
Hey Sharat,

Thanks for your reply! Your analytics background would be super valuable for this series.

I'm trying to line up all these events by the end of the month so I can get started on promo.

If the timing doesn't work out on this one, there will be other opportunities in the future :)

Cheers,
Shaw
```

**Key principles:**
- No Calendly link — don't create an obligation when fit/timing is uncertain
- Be transparent about YOUR timeline (not just accommodating about theirs)
- Always end with a future opportunity, never a dead end

### 3. Not a Fit → Polite Decline With Redirect

**When to use:** The person's background clearly doesn't match what was asked for (e.g., ML engineer responding to an analytics workshop call).

**Template:**

```
Hey [First Name],

Thanks for your reply!

[BRIEF EXPLANATION of what you're specifically looking for]

[ACKNOWLEDGE their actual strength without being evaluative]

[REDIRECT to a future opportunity that matches their background]

Cheers,
Shaw
```

**Key principles:**
- Frame the mismatch as scope ("a bit outside the scope of this particular series"), not as a judgment on them
- Reference the specific background you're looking for (data analytics, growth analytics, product analytics)
- Always redirect to a future opportunity — never dead-end the relationship
- Keep it warm. These people raised their hand; respect that.

### 4. Confused / Off-Topic Reply → Clarify

**When to use:** The person replied but seems to be responding to something different (e.g., asking to join a bootcamp instead of lead a workshop).

**Approach:**
- Ask a single short clarifying question
- Don't assume what they meant
- Use "-Shaw" sign-off (it's a quick reply, not a formal email)

**Example (Thanuj — wanted to join a cohort, not lead a workshop):**

```
Hi Thanuj,

Thanks for your reply!

Are you referring to the AI Builders Bootcamp?

-Shaw
```

Then once clarified, redirect appropriately (e.g., waitlist link for self-paced version).

### 5. Self-Selected Out → Acknowledge + Redirect

**When to use:** The person explicitly said they're not the right fit for this one.

**Approach:**
- Thank them for the reply
- Acknowledge gracefully ("All good")
- Point to a future opportunity that aligns with their actual background
- Keep it very short (2-4 lines)
- Use "-Shaw" sign-off

**Example (Justis — passed because background isn't in pure analytics):**

```
Thanks for your reply Justis!

All good, there is a tentative plan to do another series on AI for customer acquisition in May, which might align with your sales background :)

-Shaw
```

## Batch Drafting Notes

When drafting replies to multiple expert network respondents at once:

- **Use a consistent template.** Don't force variation across recipients for the sake of it. The same clean template sent to 6 people is better than 6 slightly different versions.
- **Group by response category first.** Sort people into the categories above before drafting.
- **Create as Gmail draft replies in the original thread** using the threadId from the incoming message.
- **Only personalize where earned.** Most people in a batch will get the clean template. That's fine.
