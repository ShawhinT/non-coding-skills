## Example: AI Tool Spending (Builder's Log — longer items with personality)

Why I pay $160/mo for AI tools…
(and how I use them)

1) ChatGPT - $20/mo
Best native voice-to-text, great for long brain dumps and brainstorming sessions.
Key unlock was connecting it to my Notion account so it can automatically grab context re: strategy, new/old offerings, LinkedIn posts, etc., so I don't need to reexplain every time.

2) Claude - $100/mo
Claude Code is my go-to coding agent, but more recently I've been using it to automate non-coding tasks e.g. sales outreach, keynote generation, drafting emails, and editing videos!
What sets Claude apart (IMO) is its ability to use tools, which is way beyond what I see from other frontier models (which is why I'll probably stick with the Max plan longer than expected).

3) Cursor - $20/mo
I originally used Cursor's AI panel before I picked up Claude Code and kept it around since I'd often hit my Claude Pro Plan usage limits.
While it's still nice to have the option to use different models when Claude gets confused about stuff, I haven't needed to since I upgraded to Claude Max (so I might cancel Cursor 😬)

4) Gemini - $20/mo
Gemini has the best (and fastest) tooling for image generation right now.
My main use cases are creating realistic photos of myself for YouTube thumbnails (huge) and creating infographics (sometimes).

## Example: 5 Levels of AI Maturity (Framework Post)

Companies aren't failing at AI because they lack tools.
They're failing because they lack a roadmap.

Here's the 5 levels of AI maturity I see in the wild.

Level 1: Access
Giving the right people access to the right tools.
For example, Claude Code for devs, Lovable for product people, and ChatGPT for everyone.

Level 2: Adoption
People actually use AI tools in their daily work.
This means changing behavior, not just procurement.

Level 3: Redesign (Internal)
Refactor internal workflows and processes with AI.
For example, build custom apps that speed up tedious data entry or ingestion, connect AI agents with your internal data sources and tools, etc.

Level 4: AI-enabled Delivery (External)
Use AI tools to deliver value to customers better, faster, cheaper.
This isn't integrating AI into the product (yet), just using it to drive greater impact, e.g., developers use coding agents to launch features faster, or consultants use research agents to generate better market analysis reports.

Level 5: AI-powered Offerings
Integrate AI into your core product or service.
Using AI is one thing, but building customer-facing AI tools is another. This requires developing in-house AI engineering talent and infrastructure.

What level of maturity would make the greatest impact in your organization?

## Example: AI Strategy (Framework Post — short, punchy)

Most organizations say they have an AI strategy, when what they actually have is a list of tools.

While this is a necessary step, ultimately, tools ≠ transformation.

Here's how I see it:

AI strategy is about answering a simple question:
→ What activities do we prioritize to reach our desired level of maturity?

This consists of three key steps.

Step 1: Define an AI maturity framework
Step 2: Measure the gap between the org's current & desired level of maturity
Step 3: Make a plan for overcoming the gap
