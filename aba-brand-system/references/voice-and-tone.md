# ABA Voice & Tone

ABA copy is **founder-to-founder**. Shaw is the implied speaker; the reader is a busy founder-CEO who already uses AI. Plain, declarative, assumes the reader is smart and short on time.

## Hard rules

- **Direct second person.** "You don't need to become an AI expert. You need AI doing real work." Not "users will" or "businesses can."
- **Sentence case for headings.** "Is this for you?", "Frequently asked questions." Title Case only for proper nouns ("AI Builder Academy", "1:1 Workshop", "Done-For-You Transformation").
- **Two-clause sentences max.** Short and declarative. "Half your week disappears into admin while strategy waits."
- **Concrete over abstract.** "$50K and three months of consulting that generated little ROI" beats "frustrating prior experiences."
- **No exclamation points** outside small UI states ("Free. No spam.").
- **No emoji** on marketing pages. The 🥖 in the footer "Built with 🥖 by Shaw Talebi" is the **only** exception — a personal signature, never a pattern to repeat anywhere else.
- **No AI-hype words.** Forbidden list below.

## Forbidden vocabulary

These words signal "brand wrote this." Cut them on sight.

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| unlock (figurative) | put to work, install, hand off |
| supercharge | speed up, replace |
| revolutionize / transformative | change how you work, replace the manual version |
| paradigm / paradigm shift | the new way |
| leverage (as verb) | use, run, put to work |
| seamless | quiet, in the background |
| cutting-edge / state-of-the-art | what's working today |
| game-changer / game-changing | (just say what it does) |
| AI-powered | (just say what it does — "an assistant that…") |
| next-level / level up | better, faster, fewer steps |
| empower | (don't use; just describe the outcome) |
| journey / transformation (as buzzword) | engagement, project |
| frictionless / effortless | quick, simple |
| world-class | (cut entirely; show, don't claim) |

Also banned: "Click here", "Submit", "Learn more about our services", "Sign up today!", "Limited time offer."

## CTA microcopy library

CTAs read like **the next thing you'd say in person**, with a trailing `→` arrow as a literal glyph in the button label (not an icon).

- Primary on hero: `"Book intro call →"`
- Secondary primary: `"Get in touch →"`
- Conversational: `"Let's talk."`
- Mid-funnel CTA body: `"Book a 25-minute call. We'll find one workflow worth handing off this week."`
- Footer microcopy: `"Free. No spam. Unsubscribe anytime."`
- Newsletter heading (footer column): `STAY IN THE LOOP` (uppercase Manrope, `0.2em` tracking)

## The hero pattern

Every marketing hero on the site uses the same four-part structure:

1. **Eyebrow** — uppercase Manrope 600, `0.2em` tracking, color `#087CA7`. Examples: `TRANSFORM YOUR BUSINESS`, `LET'S TALK`, `TRUSTED BY LEADERS AND BUILDERS FROM`.
2. **2-line H1 with one phrase highlighted.** The highlight is a manual underline rendered behind the text (the `.highlight` utility — primary-blue at 90% opacity, 0.25em tall, sits behind the baseline).
3. **1-sentence subhead** in body color at 60% opacity.
4. **One primary CTA** — pill button with a trailing `→`. No secondary button on the hero.

### Hero examples to lift

- Headline: `"AI Lets Small Teams Do Big Things."` (highlight on `Big Things`)
- Subhead: `"Stop researching AI tools. Start handing off work to them."`
- Eyebrow: `TRANSFORM YOUR BUSINESS`
- CTA: `"Book intro call →"`

## Pain-point cards

Titled like **complaints**, not features. The header reads as something the reader has already said to themselves.

### Do

- "You're only scratching the surface."
- "You've been burned before."
- "You don't know what to focus on."
- "Your team uses AI, but the business doesn't."
- "Half your week disappears into admin."

### Don't

- "Underutilized AI capabilities"
- "Past consulting disappointments"
- "Strategic clarity gaps"
- "Operational inefficiencies"

After the title, **one sentence** of amplification in muted body color (`rgba(255,255,255,0.5)`). Examples:

- "Spent $50K and three months on AI consulting that generated little ROI."
- "New AI tools come out every week, but you have no idea what's actually worth your time."
- "ChatGPT for emails is great. But the workflow that's costing you 20 hours a week? Still manual."

## FAQ phrasing

Questions are written **exactly how a real prospect would type them**, not how a marketer would phrase them.

### Do

- "Do I need to be technical?"
- "We already use AI day-to-day. Is this still useful?"
- "What's the time commitment?"
- "Is this a course, a coaching call, or done-for-you?"
- "How is this different from hiring a consultant?"

### Don't

- "Technical prerequisites and onboarding"
- "Differentiation from competing services"
- "Time investment expectations"

Answers are direct, ~2-4 sentences, body color at 50% opacity, leading-relaxed.

## Numbers and credentials

**Spelled out in body copy**, not in iconified stat cards.

- ✅ "Ex-Toyota Data Scientist with 8+ years in AI. Teaching over 100k builders."
- ✅ "165 grads. 4.7★ from 75 reviews."
- ❌ A 4-column row of cards with icons + numbers + labels.

## Social proof

**Logos, not metrics.** A muted brightness-0/invert marquee of company wordmarks appears after the hero. Specific brand names (Google, Microsoft, Meta, AWS, NCSL, CEI, Matson) recur in the "About Shaw" section. Numbers ("4.7★ from 75 reviews", "165 grads") appear inline in body copy, never as decorative stat cards.

## Casing & punctuation

- **Sentence case** for all headings, including h1 (`"AI Lets Small Teams Do Big Things."` is a complete sentence — note the period — but the convention for shorter headings is sentence case without trailing punctuation: `"Is this for you?"`, `"Frequently asked questions"`).
- **Title Case** is reserved for proper nouns and product names (`AI Builder Academy`, `1:1 Workshop`, `Team Workshop`, `Done-For-You Transformation`).
- **Em-dashes** are fine in body copy. No spaces around them ("founder-CEO — busy" not "founder-CEO -- busy").
- **Periods** at the end of pain-card headers (they're statements) but not at the end of section headings.

## Channel modifiers

The voice doesn't change much across surfaces — it adapts in length and density.

| Surface | Tweak |
|---|---|
| Landing pages | Full voice. Short paragraphs, generous whitespace. |
| Decks / slides | Strip even more. One idea per slide. Use eyebrow + headline + sentence subhead pattern. |
| Emails | Slightly warmer. First name OK in salutation. Same vocabulary rules. |
| Social graphics | Headline + 1 line max. Eyebrow optional. Always the cerulean accent. |
| Buttons / chips / badges | Plain working language. No clever wordplay. |

## The 5-second smell test

Before shipping copy, ask:

1. Would Shaw actually say this in a 1:1 call?
2. Could I cut a sentence and lose nothing?
3. Is there an exclamation point that shouldn't be there?
4. Did I use any word from the forbidden list?
5. Does the CTA read like the next thing I'd say in conversation?

If any answer is wrong, rewrite.
